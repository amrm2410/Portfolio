## Project Overview

Crew is an Egyptian HR consultancy firm offering five core services: outsourcing, consulting, government compliance, learning &amp; development, and recruitment. The website needed to clearly communicate this breadth of expertise while making it easy for potential clients — typically operations and HR managers — to understand which services fit their needs and take action.

---

## The Challenge

Designing a corporate website for a multi-service consultancy introduces specific challenges:

- **Service differentiation:** Five distinct service verticals (outsourcing, consulting, compliance, L&amp;D, recruitment) that overlap conceptually — users need clear boundaries and use cases for each
- **Trust and credibility:** HR and compliance services require high trust signals — the website must communicate expertise and reliability immediately
- **Bilingual content:** Arabic and English support with consistent reading experience, requiring typography that works well in both directions
- **Conversion clarity:** Each service page must guide visitors from understanding to action (inquiry submission) without aggressive sales patterns

## Research &amp; Discovery

### Target Persona

**Samir El-Masry** — Operations/HR Manager, 38 years old, Cairo

**Goals:**
- Streamline HR operations and reduce overhead costs
- Ensure 100% compliance with Egyptian labor and social insurance laws
- Improve employee development and retention
- Reduce hiring times with qualified candidates

**Pain points:**
- Navigating complex Egyptian labor laws without dedicated legal/compliance staff
- Overloaded internal HR team that can&rsquo;t keep up with regulatory paperwork
- Government interactions that are slow and error-prone
- No internal infrastructure for employee training programs

This persona informed every information architecture and content decision — the website speaks directly to mid-level managers who are overwhelmed by HR complexity and looking for a reliable partner.

## Key Design Decisions &amp; Tradeoffs

### 1. Service-first information architecture (vs. company-first)

**Decision:** Structure the website around the five services as primary navigation items, with company/about as secondary.

**Tradeoff:** Company story is less prominent on first visit.

**Why:** Visitors arrive with a specific need (compliance help, recruitment, outsourcing). Getting them to the relevant service page fast matters more than telling Crew&rsquo;s story first.

### 2. Dedicated page per service vertical

**Decision:** Each of the five services gets its own detailed page rather than a single services overview.

**Tradeoff:** More pages to design and maintain (22 total screens).

**Why:** Each service has distinct value propositions, use cases, and CTAs. A single overview page can&rsquo;t do justice to the depth of each offering and would weaken conversion.

### 3. Tajawal typeface for bilingual readability

**Decision:** Use Tajawal as the primary typeface — designed for both Arabic and English text.

**Tradeoff:** Less typographic variety compared to using separate fonts per language.

**Why:** Ensures consistent reading experience across languages without font-switching artifacts, and Tajawal&rsquo;s design quality works well in corporate contexts.

### 4. Conversion flow embedded in service pages

**Decision:** Integrate inquiry CTAs contextually within service pages rather than relying on a generic contact page.

**Tradeoff:** CTAs appear on multiple pages, requiring consistent but non-intrusive placement.

**Why:** When a visitor understands a specific service, the action should be immediate — not require navigation to a separate contact page where context is lost.

## Solution Overview

### Information Architecture

The website is structured around five service pillars with supporting pages:

- **Homepage:** Hero with value proposition, service overview cards, trust indicators, and client testimonials
- **Outsourcing:** Personnel outsourcing solutions — reducing operational overhead
- **Consulting:** Strategic business consultancy for organizational goals
- **Government Compliance:** Social insurance, labor law, and regulatory services
- **Learning &amp; Development:** Professional training programs and employee development
- **Recruitment:** Candidate sourcing, screening, and placement services
- **About / Contact:** Company story, team, and inquiry forms

### Design Patterns

- **Service cards:** Consistent structure across all five services — description, key benefits, use cases, and CTA
- **Trust signals:** Client logos, compliance certifications, and case references
- **Inquiry forms:** Context-aware forms that pre-select the relevant service based on the page the user came from

---

## System Thinking

### States &amp; Edge Cases

- **Form validation:** Real-time field validation with clear error messages in both Arabic and English
- **Empty content sections:** Graceful handling for services with fewer case studies or testimonials
- **Mobile responsiveness:** All 22 screens optimized for mobile viewing with touch-friendly interactions
- **Language switching:** Seamless toggle between Arabic and English without page reload
